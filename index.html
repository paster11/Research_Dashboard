BlackRock Investment Institute

27 weeklies, Jan 5 – Jul 13, 2026 · Latest: "Japan bonds tell global repricing story"

Key Insights

  • Mega forces — above all AI — trump the macro cycle; "immutable economic laws" (supply chains, foreign financing of U.S. debt) keep policy extremes in check.
  • Global rates have reset structurally higher; even Japan's 10-yr JGB at 30-yr highs confirms the repricing is real. Long bonds no longer reliable diversifiers ("diversification mirage").
  • Mideast supply shock (Strait of Hormuz) added to inflation pressures that pre-dated the conflict; markets differentiated rather than disconnected — record stocks alongside high oil and yields.
  • AI is a "supercharged" mega force: buildout capex rising from historic levels, revenues now tangible, bottlenecks (power, chips, data centers) are the opportunity.

Key Takeaways (positioning)

  • Overweight U.S. equities on AI-led earnings strength outpacing higher rate expectations; EM equities cut to neutral as dollar strengthened.
  • Underweight long U.S. Treasuries and JGBs; prefer front-end/belly of U.S. and euro curves, U.S. agency MBS (OW), EM local-currency debt (OW).
  • In credit: selective — higher-rated high yield, clear cash flows; downgraded broad HY strategically in May in favor of taking growth risk via stocks.
  • Favor unique diversifiers: active returns, macro hedge funds, private markets, infrastructure.

Key Outlook

  • Higher-for-longer regime: inflation settles above pre-pandemic levels; markets price a possible Fed HIKE by end-2026 (new chair Warsh dropped forward guidance).
  • Durable income is back — 80%+ of global fixed income yields above 4% — but "not all income is equal"; selectivity over broad indices.
  • Japan: prefer equities over JGBs as reflation and BoJ normalization proceed.

Key Monitoring

  • U.S. CPI/PCE — energy & producer cost pass-through to core; whether Fed hike gets confirmed.
  • Crowded short-yen positioning / carry-trade unwind risk if BoJ shifts abruptly.
  • Strait of Hormuz reopening durability; U.S.–Iran deal headlines.
  • AI earnings breadth vs. higher rates — the central equity trade-off.

Invesco Solutions — Tactical Asset Allocation

April & July 2026 editions · Latest: July 1, 2026

Key Insights

  • Macro framework in a SLOWDOWN regime: growth above trend but decelerating; risk appetite modestly decelerating after strong AI-fueled returns.
  • Regime driver has shifted: from inflation uncertainty (April, energy shock) to policy constraint — falling inflation expectations with rising REAL rates = tighter financial conditions.
  • Q1-26 fundamentals strong: 85% of S&P 500 beat EPS; IT earnings +54%; positive guidance above 5- and 10-yr norms.
  • Hormuz reopened (~18 tankers/day vs. pre-conflict norm) but damaged energy infrastructure keeps supply risk alive.

Key Takeaways (positioning)

  • Moderate overweight equities vs. fixed income — but defensively expressed: OW quality & low volatility factors; favor IT, health care, staples over cyclicals.
  • Regional: incrementally reduced U.S. preference vs. DM ex-US, and DM vs. EM.
  • Fixed income: underweight credit risk; overweight duration as a growth hedge; now favor nominal Treasuries over inflation-linked.
  • USD flipped to underweight; favor EUR, JPY, CAD, NOK, SGD and high-yielding EM FX (INR, IDR, TWD, THB).

Key Outlook

  • Slowdown is a risk-management posture, not a cycle-ending signal; deceleration continues into H2.
  • Higher real yields are a headwind for growth assets and raise the odds the slowdown persists.
  • Rate convergence globally erodes USD carry appeal — supports non-US assets.

Key Monitoring

  • Leading economic indicators & global risk appetite (regime-change triggers).
  • Hormuz tanker traffic and energy-infrastructure rebuild timeline.
  • Q2 earnings season follow-through on AI super-cycle momentum.
  • Real-rate trajectory / financial-conditions tightening.

Morgan Stanley IM — The BEAT

2026 Outlook, Q2 (May), Q3 (July) · Latest: Q3 2026

Key Insights

  • Core thesis: "Adapting to a structurally higher nominal world" — the question isn't growth collapse, it's whether investors adapt to structurally higher nominal growth and rates.
  • The Mideast disruption was a PRICE shock, not a VALUATION shock (Q2 framing) — dislocation, not deterioration; that view was vindicated as oil fell post-MOU.
  • Equities are the prime beneficiary of higher nominal activity; earnings, not multiples, drive returns.
  • Fixed income alpha requires active duration/credit management — blend IG, HY, EMD; floating-rate & ABS add flexibility.

Key Takeaways (positioning)

  • Top views: OW U.S. equities (AI tailwinds, resilient growth), OW U.S. SMID, NEW: OW "AI Winners" (picks & shovels, agentic enablers).
  • Europe downgraded to underweight vs. U.S. — keep only Banks & Utilities/grid.
  • Duration slightly UW with curve-steepening bias; credit UW (spreads near record tights) — prefer MBS/ABS (highest-conviction bond OW), bank loans, HY over IG, EMD hard currency.
  • Overall equity risk NEUTRAL at the allocation level; OW cyclicals vs. defensives in U.S.; OW TIPS on rising inflation expectations.

Key Outlook

  • Inflation skews above target; real growth stays firm on labor markets, productivity, capex — supportive of risk assets.
  • Geopolitical risk becoming more manageable — U.S.-Iran MOU expected to hold; easing tail risks supports sentiment and broadening breadth.
  • Growth broadening beyond mega caps → SMID and industrial recovery theme.

Key Monitoring

  • Durability of the U.S.–Iran MOU and Hormuz traffic normalization.
  • Oil futures curve (backwardation) — transient vs. sustained shock signal.
  • Demand destruction signs: jobs market, consumption.
  • Credit spreads / defaults as systemic-stress early warning.

Consensus vs. Divergence

Where all three agree

  • • Structurally higher rates/nominal regime — underweight or cautious on long duration & IG credit at tight spreads.
  • • U.S. equity earnings (AI-driven) remain the core engine; income via securitized/selective credit over broad IG.
  • • Mideast shock = manageable; markets right to look through it, but Hormuz durability is the shared watch-item.

Where they diverge

  • • Risk appetite: BlackRock pro-risk / OW U.S. equities; Invesco OW-but-defensive and trimming the U.S.; MS neutral overall risk with U.S. OW inside.
  • • Duration: Invesco OW duration as a slowdown hedge — directly opposite BlackRock's UW long bonds and MS's slight UW.
  • • Europe: MS downgraded to UW just as Invesco tilts toward DM ex-US; BlackRock sits neutral.
  • • Inflation-linked: MS OW TIPS (expects inflation expectations to rise) vs Invesco rotating out of linkers into nominals.
